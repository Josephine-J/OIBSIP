Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 – 2015

“You have to dream before your dreams can come true.”

Biography

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the “Missile Man of India”, was one of the nation’s most respected scientists and the 11th President of India. Born in Rameswaram, he rose from humble beginnings to lead India’s space and missile development programs, contributing greatly to ISRO and DRDO.

His vision for India, his simplicity, and his inspiring speeches made him a hero for millions. Dr. Kalam believed in the power of youth and spent his life motivating students to dream big. He authored several books, including “Wings of Fire”, which continues to inspire generations.

Even after serving as President, he remained a teacher at heart, working tirelessly for education and innovation until his final day.
